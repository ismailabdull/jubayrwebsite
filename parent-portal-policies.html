Policies & Expectations

Understanding our policies helps create a positive learning environment for all students and families.

School Policies & Guidelines

üëî Dress Code

We maintain a modest and respectful dress code that reflects Islamic values and creates a focused learning environment.

For Boys:

  • Modest, clean clothing (no shorts above the knee)
  • No clothing with inappropriate graphics or text
  • Clean, well-maintained appearance
  • Hats and caps are not allowed indoors

For Girls:

  • Modest, loose-fitting clothing
  • Long skirts or pants (ankle-length)
  • Long-sleeved tops
  • Hijab is encouraged but not mandatory
  • No tight or revealing clothing
Note: Students who do not follow the dress code may be asked to change or may be sent home. We appreciate your cooperation in maintaining our Islamic values.

üìö Behavior & Classroom Rules

We expect all students to demonstrate respect, responsibility, and Islamic manners in all interactions.

Classroom Expectations:

  • Arrive on time and be prepared for class
  • Listen attentively and participate respectfully
  • Raise your hand before speaking
  • Complete assignments on time
  • Respect teachers, staff, and fellow students
  • Use appropriate language and tone
  • Keep the classroom clean and organized

Prohibited Behaviors:

  • Bullying, teasing, or harassment of any kind
  • Use of inappropriate language or gestures
  • Disrupting class or other students' learning
  • Bringing electronic devices (unless required for class)
  • Eating or drinking during class time
Important: We follow Islamic principles of kindness, respect, and good character. Students are expected to embody these values at all times.

üìÖ Attendance Guidelines

Regular attendance is crucial for academic success and maintaining consistency in Islamic education.

Attendance Requirements:

  • Students are expected to attend all scheduled classes
  • Arrive 5-10 minutes before class begins
  • Notify the school in advance if absence is necessary
  • Make up missed work within one week of return

Absence Policy:

  • Excused absences: illness, family emergency, religious holidays
  • Unexcused absences: skipping class, oversleeping, social events
  • Excessive absences may result in academic probation
  • Students with 3+ consecutive absences will receive a phone call home

Tardiness:

  • Students arriving more than 10 minutes late will be marked tardy
  • 3 tardies = 1 unexcused absence
  • Repeated tardiness may result in parent conference

‚öñÔ∏è Discipline Policy

Our discipline approach is based on Islamic principles of guidance, correction, and positive reinforcement.

Progressive Discipline Steps:

  1. Verbal Warning: First instance of minor misbehavior
  2. Written Warning: Repeated minor issues or first major incident
  3. Parent Conference: Continued behavioral issues
  4. Suspension: Serious violations or repeated problems
  5. Expulsion: Extreme cases or continued violations

Positive Reinforcement:

  • Recognition of good behavior and academic achievement
  • Student of the month awards
  • Special privileges for consistent good behavior
  • Parent communication about positive progress
Our Goal: We work with families to help students develop self-discipline and Islamic character. Most issues are resolved through communication and guidance.

Questions About Our Policies?

We're here to help clarify any policies or discuss individual circumstances. Please don't hesitate to reach out to us.
